Doula Interview Questions

First baby • Virginia Hospital Center (Arlington) • Zoom-friendly checklist
Tip: Ask the prompt line out loud, then pick 3–5 questions per section.
Use: Check chemistry + hospital experience + partner support.
Focus: Calm, practical, hospital-savvy
Includes: TENS unit questions

⌕

1) Getting to Know You

“We’re having our first baby, so we’d love to hear a little about you.”
  1. How did you become a doula?
  2. How long have you been doing this?
  3. About how many births have you attended?
  4. How much of your work is with first-time parents?

2) Your Experience at Virginia Hospital Center

“We’ll be delivering at VHC, so we’re curious about your experience there.”
  1. Have you attended births at VHC before?
  2. What is the labor and delivery environment like there?
  3. How do the nurses and OBs usually interact with doulas?
  4. Are there any hospital routines at VHC that we should be aware of ahead of time?

3) How You Work With First-Time Parents

“We’re excited, but also a little nervous since this is all new to us.”
  1. How do you support people who have never given birth before?
  2. How do you help us prepare mentally and emotionally for labor?
  3. How do you help us understand our options when decisions come up?
  4. What does “informed consent” mean in your work?

4) Your Birth Philosophy

“We’re hoping for a calm, supportive hospital birth.”
  1. How do you support low-intervention births in a medical setting?
  2. How do you feel about epidurals and pain medication?
  3. How do you handle it if plans change during labor?
  4. How do you advocate for us while still working well with the hospital staff?

5) Supporting Both of Us

“We want this to feel like something we’re doing together.”
  1. How do you support the non-birthing partner during labor?
  2. How do you help partners know what to do and how to help?
  3. What do you do if one of us is feeling overwhelmed or anxious?
  4. How do you make sure we still feel in control of our birth?

6) Labor Support & Comfort

“We’d love to know what you actually do during labor.”
  1. What kinds of comfort measures do you use most?
  2. How do you help with things like back labor or long inductions?
  3. How do you know when it’s time to switch techniques?
  4. How do you support someone with an epidural?
  5. How do you support someone without one?

7) TENS Unit

“We’re interested in using a TENS unit for pain management.”
  1. Do you provide a TENS unit?
  2. Is it included in your fee?
  3. Do you help with placement, timing, and adjusting it during labor?
  4. Have you used TENS units in hospital births and inductions?

8) If Things Don’t Go as Planned

“We know first births can be unpredictable.”
  1. How do you support us if an induction is recommended?
  2. What do you do if labor stalls?
  3. How do you support us if a C-section becomes necessary?
  4. Are you able to come to the OR if allowed?
  5. How do you help us process a difficult or unexpected birth?

9) After the Baby Is Born

“We want support in those first important moments.”
  1. Do you stay for the golden hour?
  2. How do you help with the first latch and breastfeeding?
  3. What postpartum visits are included?
  4. How do you support emotional recovery after birth?

10) Availability & Backup

“We want to make sure we’re covered.”
  1. How many clients do you take around our due date?
  2. Who is your backup if you’re at another birth?
  3. How do we reach you when labor starts?
  4. When do you usually come to the hospital?

11) Fit & Logistics

“We’re really trying to find the right match.”
  1. What kinds of families do you work best with?
  2. What kinds of families might not be a great fit?
  3. What’s a birth you’re especially proud of?
  4. Why do you do this work?

12) Fees & Contract

“Let’s talk logistics.”
  1. What’s included in your fee?
  2. When is payment due?
  3. What’s your refund or cancellation policy?
  4. What happens if we end up with a scheduled C-section?
  5. Are you insured and certified?
Quick close-out prompt:
“Is there anything you think first-time parents often overlook that you’d recommend we plan for?”
